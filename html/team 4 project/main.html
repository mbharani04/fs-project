    11. GUIDELINES

1. FINANCIAL STRESS

 Impacts:
  • I may have less time to focus on studying and completing assignments because of my part-time job.
  • Working long hours can make me feel tired and reduce my ability to concentrate on my studies.
  • Managing both work and academic responsibilities can sometimes create stress and affect my mental health.
  • If I work more than 20 hours per week, it can increase my academic pressure and make balancing my schedule more difficult.


2. ANXIETY AND DEPRESSION

Impacts:
  • Academic pressure and fear of exams.
  • Lack of proper time management.
  • Stress from assignments, records, and deadlines.
  • Comparing ourselves with others.
  • Overuse of social media.
  • Lack of proper sleep and unhealthy daily routine.
  • Fear about future career and job opportunities.
  • Lack of emotional support or guidance.

3. ACADEMIC PRESSURE

 Impacts:
  • Late announcements about assignments and record submissions create pressure for students.
  • Delaying the writing of records until the last minute increases stress.
  • Not practicing properly in the lab due to unnecessary talking with friends.
  • Not completing observations or experiments correctly during lab sessions.
  • Simply copying and pasting from the lab manual instead of understanding the experiment.
  • Poor time management during academic activities.
  • Lack of focus on practical learning during laboratory classes.

4. TIME MANAGEMENT

 Impacts:
  • Not setting proper goals for academic and personal development.
  • Wasting too much time on social media.
  • Not giving priority to short-term and long-term goals.
  • Making too many commitments, such as joining multiple online courses at the same time.
  • Spending excessive time on social media and responding to unnecessary messages or content.
  • Waking up late in the morning and rushing to college.
  • Feeling rushed during important tasks due to poor time management.
  • Skipping important work or responsibilities.

5. RELATIONSHIPS

Impacts:
  • Misunderstandings between friends or classmates.
  • Lack of proper communication in relationships.
  • Conflicts or arguments with close friends.
  • Spending too much time in relationships and losing focus on studies.
  • Emotional stress caused by relationship problems.
  • Feeling distracted from academic goals.
  • Peer pressure from friends.
  • Difficulty balancing personal relationships and academic responsibilities.
  • Talking or gossiping with untrustworthy people.
  • Sharing personal matters with people who cannot be trusted.
  • Trusting others without properly understanding their intentions.

6. CAREER UNCERTAINTY

 Impacts:
  • Making wrong decisions due to lack of proper career guidance.
  • Failing to set clear career goals.
  • Ignoring the importance of skill development during college.
  • Not securing internships during the early years of study.
  • Waiting until the final year to start thinking seriously about career plans.
  • Choosing short-term courses that are not interesting or useful.
  • Being careless about future career opportunities.
  • Lack of awareness about industry requirements and job market trends.
  • Comparing career paths with friends instead of focusing on personal interests.
  • Fear of failure while trying new opportunities.

7. EXAM TENSION

 Impacts:
  • Not understanding the concepts properly during the semester.
  • Fear of failure in exams.
  • Pressure from parents, teachers, and personal expectations.
  • Feeling stressed the night before the exam.
  • Not getting proper sleep before the exam day.
  • Lack of proper preparation and revision.
  • Poor time management during study time.
  • Comparing performance with other students.
  • Last-minute studying and panic.
  • Lack of confidence in one's preparation.

8. HARASSMENT

 Impacts:
  • Bullying or teasing by classmates or seniors.
  • Verbal harassment such as insulting or humiliating comments.
  • Cyberbullying through social media or messaging platforms.
  • Discrimination based on gender, background, or appearance.
  • Lack of support from peers or authorities when facing harassment.
  • Fear of reporting the issue due to possible consequences.
  • Feeling isolated or uncomfortable in the college environment.
  • Unwanted comments, jokes, or behavior that make students feel unsafe.

9. HEALTH ISSUES

Impacts:
  • Lack of proper sleep due to late-night studying or excessive screen time.
  • Unhealthy eating habits and skipping meals.
  • Eating too much junk food instead of healthy meals.
  • Stress and mental fatigue caused by academic pressure.
  • Spending long hours sitting and using laptops or mobile phones.
  • Lack of regular physical exercise.
  • Headaches, eye strain, and body pain due to continuous study or screen usage.
  • Weak immune system caused by an unhealthy lifestyle.
  • Ignoring health problems due to busy academic schedules.

Conclusion: Is College Worth It All?

In my college, one of the best rules was that mobile phones were not allowed in class. This helped students focus more on their studies and participate in academic activities. However, at the same time, I was facing financial pressure, so I started doing a part-time job. During that period, I did not think much about other challenges in my college life. I believed that being with the right group of friends would help me manage everything. During my first year, college life felt very similar to school. We were expected to maintain attendance and complete all the assigned work regularly. There was not much motivation or encouragement from others, but I continued attending classes and completing my responsibilities. In my second and third years, many students started taking classes less seriously. During class time, students were often busy with their own activities such as using phones, chatting on social media, or bunking classes. Because of this environment, a lot of valuable time was wasted. At that time, I believed that I could focus on career development in my final year, so I did not pay much attention to internships or skill development during the third year. Later, I realized how much time had been wasted during those years. Another challenge was that some staff members did not come to class on time, and there was very little guidance about future career paths. Even when advice was given, there was no clear first step or proper direction on how to begin. Teachers always emphasized the importance of attendance and avoiding phone usage in class. However, without access to phones or laptops, it was sometimes difficult to explore new topics or learn additional skills. Overall, these experiences made me realize the importance of using college time wisely and focusing on personal growth and career preparation. This all made me to think is the college worth it all
