Introduction About Me:

I am Gowtham. this website is about my college experience.Even though I don’t have many memories yet,I want to explain my experiences,and the lessons I learned during my college life.


Anxiety and depression:

During my college life, I sometimes feel anxiety and stress because of different reasons. Exams, assignments, and the pressure to get good marks can make me feel worried. I also think about my future and career, which sometimes makes me feel nervous. Adjusting to the college environment and meeting new people was not always easy for me. Sometimes the expectations from family and teachers also add more pressure. Because of this, I may feel mentally tired or lose motivation for a short time. During these moments, I try to talk with my friends or family to feel better. I also take small breaks and spend time doing things I enjoy. Managing my time properly helps me stay calm and continue focusing on my studies.


College life cycle of Balance:

In my college life, I try to maintain a balance between my studies, personal life, and other activities. Sometimes it becomes difficult for me to manage everything like classes, assignments, and my personal interests. If I do not plan my time properly, I can feel stressed or tired. That is why I understand that time management is very important for me during college life. I try to organize my time so that I can focus on my studies and also have some time to relax. I also spend time doing activities that I enjoy. For example, I like playing cricket during the weekends because it helps me relax and refresh my mind. Keeping this balance helps me stay happy and focus better on my studies.


Academic performance:

Academic performance is an important part of my college life. It shows how well I understand the subjects that are taught in my classes. I consider myself an average student, but I always try to improve my knowledge and skills. During lectures, I try to pay attention and understand the concepts clearly. I also try to complete my assignments on time because it helps me learn the topics better. Studying regularly helps me remember what I learned and perform better in exams. For me, academic success is not only about getting high marks but also about gaining knowledge. I believe that with hard work and dedication, I can slowly improve my performance. My college education is also helping me develop skills that will support my future career.


Financial problem:

Financial problems are something I also think about during my college life. Education expenses such as tuition fees, books, and transportation can sometimes be difficult for me and my family to manage. Because of this, I sometimes feel worried about my studies and future. Financial stress can affect my concentration and make it harder to focus on my studies. It also makes me realize the value of money and the effort my family puts into supporting my education. I try to manage my expenses carefully and avoid unnecessary spending. In the future, I may also look for scholarships or other financial support if needed. These experiences are teaching me how to handle financial responsibility during my college life.

sports:

Cricket is my favorite sport and it is an important part of my college life. I really enjoy playing cricket because it helps me relax and forget about my academic pressure for some time. When I play cricket with my friends, I feel more energetic and happy. It also helps me reduce stress after spending many hours studying or attending classes. Cricket teaches me teamwork, discipline, and how to stay focused during the game. I usually play cricket during the weekends, and it refreshes my mind after a busy week. Playing cricket keeps me active and improves my physical fitness. For me, cricket is not just a game, it is also a way to enjoy my college life and stay mentally fresh.

Disadvantages of my college:

In my opinion, there are some disadvantages in my college that affect my overall college experience. One of the main problems is that there are no cultural programs or events conducted for students. Because of this, we do not get many opportunities to show our talents or enjoy activities outside academics. Another issue is the short college timing, which sometimes feels like there is not enough time for proper learning, interaction with teachers, or participation in activities. I also feel that the college does not always give enough care or attention to students and their problems. Sometimes students may need guidance or support, but it feels like it is not given much importance. Another thing I notice is that the college seems very focused on money, which makes it feel more money-minded rather than student-focused. Because of these reasons, I sometimes feel that the college experience could be better if there was more attention given to students and more activities organized for us.
